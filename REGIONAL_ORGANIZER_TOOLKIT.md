## ONE-PAGE OVERVIEW

### What is EGD2026?
The annual innovation festival for MENA & Africa. On September 15, 2026, thousands of builders, researchers, founders, and students will gather across 18+ cities for a coordinated, live-bridged event.

### What is a Regional Organizer?
You host the local 12-hour EGD2026 event in your city. We provide curriculum, judges, mentorship, marketing, and infrastructure. You handle venue, local recruitment, and facilitation. You keep 50% of ticket revenue.

### Timeline
- **July 15:** Application deadline
- **July 20:** Acceptance notifications  
- **Aug 1:** Planning begins
- **Aug 15:** Marketing ramp-up
- **Sept 15:** Festival day
- **Oct 1:** Final reports + 2027 planning

### Financial Model
```
Revenue per regional hub (conservative estimate):
• 100 attendees × $25 avg ticket = $2,500 revenue
• 50% goes to Regional Organizer = $1,250 profit
• 50% goes to Elmahrosa operations = $1,250

Revenue per regional hub (optimistic):
• 200 attendees × $40 avg ticket = $8,000 revenue
• 50% goes to Regional Organizer = $4,000 profit
• 50% goes to Elmahrosa operations = $4,000
```

**Your potential earnings: $1,250 - $4,000 per event** (plus goodwill, networking, career opportunities)

## WHAT YOU PROVIDE

### Venue
- **Size:** 50-200 person capacity
- **Hours:** 12 continuous hours (9am-9pm, or similar)
- **Amenities:** 
  - Stable internet (critical)
  - Power outlets (lots)
  - Seating/tables for teams
  - Whiteboard/marker boards
  - Projector/screen
  - Bathroom facilities
  - Optional: catering, sleeping areas

**Venue Finding Resources:**
- Local universities (negotiate space)
- Co-working spaces (negotiable rate)
- Tech incubators/hubs
- Corporate offices (ask for CSR support)
- Government facilities (civic partnerships)

### Local Recruitment
- Recruit 50-200 local participants (any tier)
- Use social media, emails, word-of-mouth
- We provide templates; you customize for your market
- Target: 40% students, 40% developers, 20% founders

### Facilitation & MC
- Welcome participants (morning opening)
- Explain rules + tracks (kickoff)
- Keep time (workshops, hackathon, demos)
- Energize the room (music, breaks, motivational comments)
- Close the event (awards, thank you)

### Local Mentors/Judges
- Recruit 5-10 local mentors (industry experts)
- Recruit 3-5 judges (evaluators)
- Brief them on EGD format + evaluation criteria
- We provide mentor toolkit + judge rubrics

### Post-Event Reporting
- Collect attendance data
- Survey participants (satisfaction, feedback)
- Document highlights (photos, testimonials)
- Report to Elmahrosa (for annual review)

## COMMON QUESTIONS

### Q: What if I don't hit 100 attendees?
A: That's okay. Start small, build quality. Even 30-50 engaged participants is valuable. 
Revenue scales, but the impact is the same. We support you either way.

### Q: Can I charge sponsors for booth space?
A: Yes! We encourage local sponsorships. Keep local sponsor revenue in your pocket. 
Elmahrosa only takes 50% of participant ticket revenue.

### Q: What if my venue falls through?
A: Contact us immediately. We have university/corporate partners who can help on 
short notice.

### Q: Do I need to be technical?
A: No. You need to be organized, energetic, and community-minded. The technical 
content is ready for you.

### Q: What if registrations are slow?
A: We'll help with marketing. This is a shared goal. Reach out for extra templates, 
social media amplification, or direct outreach from Elmahrosa.

### Q: Can I do this remotely (not in-person)?
A: Not for the 2026 festival. EGD is about physical community building. But if you're 
remote, you can co-organize with a local partner on the ground.

### Q: What happens if I don't follow through?
A: We'll work it out. If life gets in the way, let us know early. We'd rather pivot 
than surprise us last minute. Honesty is part of our culture.
